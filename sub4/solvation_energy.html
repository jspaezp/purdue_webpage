Calculating Solvation Energies

In order to calculate the change in solvation free energy from Cl to F we used the slow growth method approach, and we contrasted the empirical approach of 'morphing' one molecule to another in the same run and contrasted it with the more rigurous approach of using a thermodynamic cycle.

We can see that by using the direc method (Cl to F and F to Cl in water) we are able to obtain reproducible results (193.881 vs 193.418 Kj/mol) due to the simplicity of the system and the small difference in the two components (Cl and F), nonetheless it is to note that this approach is not the best one for larger changes in the system where intermediate equilibration steps are needed to account for the changes in the rest of the system due to the change induced in the molecule.
