Modeling of Deca-Alanine

Re-Folding of Deca Alanine

To depict the folding process of deca-alanine to its stable helical conformation, a simulation was run where a force was exerted to the molecule to induce its unfolding and afterwards, this force was removed to allow the peptide to return to its stable configuration (shown in figure 1)

Fig 1. Depiction of the refolding process of decaalanine.

Unfolding of Deca-Alanine

In addition, the unfolding of deca-alanine was simulated by exerting a pull to the extremes of the molecule of DA. This generated an unfolding of it, which can be visualized in several ways, as a first approach one can express this unfolding as a change with respect to the initial folded configuration of DA, measured by the distance between the extremes of the alanine chain, Figure 2). Nonetheless this depiction is fairly crude and much more information can be obtained through other depictions of the process, as it is the force required for each step in the deformation to occur (Figure 3), where a change in the 'force regime' occurs when all hydrogen bonds are broken and further force only strains angles and torsions.

In addition it is posible to observe the manner in which the deca-alanine is unfolding under a more 'structural biology' perspective, where the initial structure is textbook example of an alpha helix due to the torsion angles, stabilized by in-chain hydrogen bonds, therefore the unfolding of the structure implies a rupture of this bonds and a modifications of this angles. The modification of the angles and therefore change in secondary structure can be visualized in Figures 4 and 5 by the distribution of torsions in the ramachandran plot as the pulling proceeds; this can also be seen in Fiure 6 where the distribution of secondary structures is seen.
