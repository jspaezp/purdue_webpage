Molecular Docking with Candock

Docking FDA approved compounds to Target

In order to simulate and predict the binding of several compounds to a given protein we used the CANDOCK software, which differs from commonly used programs (like autodock) in the fact that it simulates the binding of the given compounds by first separating the molecules into fragments, as their 'rotatable' components and ten connects this fragments to generate a final conformation.

Scores of the docked compounds

We docked a list of compounds (provided up) to the structure of the kinase domain of FLT3 bound to quizartnib (a kinase inhibitor), and we are able to appreciate the diversity of binding affinities of multiple compounds, and more importantly we can appreciate that compounds can associate with the kinase domain without being kinase inhibitors; which would imply that the classification as a kinase inhibitor relies not on the binary binder-non binder conception but more on the strength of the binding.

In order to appreciate which interactions lead to higher binding affinities in the bindingg pocket the visualization of the structures is provided of selected compounds (up) where we can see that good binders (P30, 1N1 and IRE) show a fairly deep integration with the pocket in addition to exquisite shape complementarity, therefore we can suggest that many van der waals interactions are providing the strength of binding, in contrast with bad binders that show a shallower binding; this notion is actually slightly counter-intuitive, since one could expect the binding to occur in great measure due to a few really strong interactions (extremely evident hydrogen bonds or ionic atractions).

Comparisson with the Crystal Structure

Since one of the compounds used was the one used to crystalize the structure, we can use it to compare the predicted binding to the site with the lowest potential energy (the one that forms a crystal). We can appreciate that the predicted binding does not correspond to the binding depicted in the crystal structure, nonetheless this could be cause by several factors, since the binding occurs without molecular dynamics its posible that the lowest energy binding site is not acually the ensamble average at the temperature of crystalization. In addition it is to note that several aproximations where done in order to accelerate the docking process, as it is using only a percentage of the viable seeds, forcing a rigid docking and using only the top 20 conformations to generate an ensamble average.

Lead Optimization

In order to explore the additional capacities of candock, we ran the lead optimization protocol, where given a series of compounds, it generates combinations that would increase the binding affinities to a given protein and would reduce the binding affinity to an anti-target.
