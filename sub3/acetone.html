Modelling a molecule of acetone in water

Approach to the modelling of Acetone Or how to make acetone as simple as needed but not a single bit more

In order to simulate a single molecule of acetone in a box of water, several aproximation where mate to make its behaviour resemble the one of an actual acetone molecule, in order to reduce the computational expense involves in calculating the energy in each step of the simulation for our molecule, the hydrogens where integrated to their bound carbon as methyl groups. In addition, in order to consider the nature of the carbon that forms a double bond with oxigen, where due to its configuration forms a plannar structure two topologies where proposed, one where this plannarity is coherced by constraining the distances between non-bonded atoms and another where the dihedral angles are the ones determining the configuration of the molecule.

Distance-Constrain Topology

Note in the file how bonds are not stated in any particular functional form but in a constrains manner, where their distance cannot change, therefore the inproper distances do not differ in any manner to the actual bonds in chemical terms.
