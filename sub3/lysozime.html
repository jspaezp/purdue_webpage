Lysozyme

Lysozyme Modeling

The simulation of the protein Lysozime was carried out by using the crystal structure with accession code 1AKE deposited in the protein data bank as a template for the initial configuration (originally resolved by Artymiuk, et.al 1982). It is to note that the biochemistry and structure of this protein is widely known due to its small size, solubility in aqueous environment, simplicity to purify and abundace, which has made it a workhorse as a test subject in the developement of multiple techniques that now are commonly used to extract information from biomolecules (as x ray crystalography or biomolecular NMR), all of this also makes it appealing as a test subject for molecular dynamics simulations, since it is posible to compare results from the simulations with experimental evidence.

In order to simulate the protein in water, the structure was converted to gromacs format, solvated with 'SPC' water molecules, and subject to sequential steps of energy minimization (figure 1), NVT simulation to generate velocities (Figure 2) and finally an NPT simulation (Figure 3).

Discussing the movement of lysozyme

To evaluate the stability of our simulated protein in the system, the Root Mean Square Deviation (RMSD) with respect to the Crystal structure was measured at each time-point, where a lack of increase of this value is interpreted as a stable configuration (in addition to a really small value of merely 0.12, as depicted in Figure 6).
