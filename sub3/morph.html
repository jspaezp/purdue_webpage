Introduction Ca+2 gated channel

Most cells make use of an asymetric electrochemical potential across its cell membrane, this potential is commonly used for multiple processes such as active trasnport, driving chemical reactions or short term signals (as it is the case in neurons and muscles). Voltage gated Calcium channels are proteins found in several organisms and tisues, whose function lies in the permeation of calcium ions (normally extremelo low in concentration in the interior of the cell) after sensing a depolarizatino of the cell membrane. As such, the CavAB protein is present in cardiac muscle and plays a significant role in the contraction of cardiac musle (aka: hearbeat), therefore considering and understanding mechanism by which chemical compounds might affect this protein is of ulterior importance. Here we show how the allosteric bincing of a small molecule antagonist changes the conformatio of the protein to reduce the permeability of Ca through the channel.

Image Showing the change in conformation between CavAb without and with the added small molecule antagonist, where it is posible to note that changes are actually modest in the structure of the protein; Nonetheless there is a change of almost half an angstrom in the selectiviry region of the protein, where the intermediate state in the channel would be less stable and therefore woulb be less likely to occur, and ultimately would lead to a decrease in conductivity for this ion.

Image Showing the change in conformation between CavAb without and with the added small molecule antagonist, at the single chain level, where it is evident that the percieved movement is more due to the relative orientation of chains with each other than an actual large movment in the structure of the chain.
